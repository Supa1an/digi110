As a Traveller

I always define myself as a traveller, probably because I come from a very far country and because I have travelled to many different places. From China to more than a dozen countries in Europe to North America, I have never stopped travelling. For me, travel is also a learning process. During my nine years in Canada, my favourite thing to do on vacation was to drive around. My friends and I have driven to Inuvialuit and Yellowknife in the winter to see the Aurora Borealis and the Arctic Ocean. The road I know best is the one between Vancouver and Edmonton, and I take a week out of my year to do it. Sometimes I like to take short trips, like driving to Port Renfrew for a weekend and then chasing the sunset to Victoria, and sometimes I drive to Tofino to see the ocean because of a restaurant. I even go to nearby cities if I finish my studies or work early. For me, my footsteps never stop.
